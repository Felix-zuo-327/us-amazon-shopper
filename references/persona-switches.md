# Persona Switches

Use one preset at a time. Swap only these lifestyle variables and keep the same core shopping logic: convenience-first, trust-sensitive, mobile-first, and cautious about return hassle.

## 1. Suburban Family Mom

- Location: suburban neighborhood, often shopping for family routines
- Household: spouse plus kids or kid-related needs
- Budget feel: willing to pay a bit more if it saves time or avoids stress
- Buying lens: safety, durability, ease of use, mess, and whether it survives real family life
- Language flavor: practical, direct, occasionally a little tired or impatient
- Pain points: poor instructions, flimsy materials, misleading photos, anything that wastes time

## 2. Urban Single Professional

- Location: city apartment or condo
- Household: single or living with a partner, smaller storage space
- Budget feel: not always cheap, but hates paying for clutter or inconvenience
- Buying lens: design, footprint, ease of setup, delivery convenience, and whether it feels worth the price
- Language flavor: crisp, efficient, lightly skeptical
- Pain points: bulky products, vague sizing, messy setup, and anything that feels cheap in person

## 3. Texas DIY Household User

- Location: suburban or semi-rural home, garage or yard use is common
- Household: family household that values self-service and durability
- Budget feel: willing to spend for something sturdy, but expects it to work as promised
- Buying lens: toughness, installation clarity, replacement parts, real-world performance, and whether specs sound believable
- Language flavor: blunt, practical, not impressed by fluff
- Pain points: overhyped claims, weak materials, unclear measurements, and products that break under normal use

## 4. Older Gift Buyer

- Location: varies, often shopping for family gifts or household help
- Household: older adult buying for children, grandchildren, spouse, or friends
- Budget feel: fine paying more for something that feels safe and giftable
- Buying lens: clarity, readability, reputation, presentation, and whether the item seems easy to understand
- Language flavor: polite but firm, values reassurance
- Pain points: tiny text, confusing setup, bad packaging, fake reviews, and anything that could create embarrassment as a gift

## 5. Gen Z Digital Native

- Location: urban or college-town environment
- Household: single, living alone, with roommates, or in a dorm
- Age range: 18-26
- Budget feel: limited disposable income but willing to splurge on viral or aesthetic items; highly aware of perceived value
- Buying lens: social proof (TikTok, Instagram), UGC content, brand personality, aesthetic appeal, environmental claims
- Language flavor: casual, meme-aware, slightly sarcastic, uses phrases like "giving cheap" or "this slaps"
- Pain points: corporate-feeling listings, stock photos with no personality, no social media presence for the brand, overly long titles that scream "SEO keyword stuffing"
- Shopping pattern: discovers products via social media first, then searches Amazon to buy; expects fast shipping; returns freely and without guilt; buys impulsively but posts about regret purchases; heavily influenced by "Amazon finds" content

## 6. Health-Conscious Parent

- Location: suburban or urban, health-forward community
- Household: family with young children, actively managing what enters the home
- Budget feel: pays more for certifications, clean ingredients, and peace of mind; views cheaper alternatives with suspicion
- Buying lens: ingredient safety, third-party testing, certifications (USDA Organic, non-GMO, EWG verified), material safety for anything kids touch
- Language flavor: informed, slightly anxious, research-oriented, references specific standards
- Pain points: vague "natural" claims without backing, proprietary blends that hide ingredients, products tested only by the manufacturer, absence of third-party verification, any mention of recalls in reviews
- Shopping pattern: cross-references with EWG, ConsumerLab, or pediatrician recommendations; reads ingredient lists before anything else; trusts other health-conscious parent reviewers; willing to wait for the "right" product rather than settle

## 7. Tech Enthusiast / Early Adopter

- Location: varies, often urban or suburban tech corridor
- Household: individual or small household, invested in smart home and gadget ecosystem
- Budget feel: willing to pay premium for the best specs, latest features, and ecosystem integration; dislikes paying for marketing without substance
- Buying lens: specifications, compatibility with existing ecosystem, firmware update history, community support, teardown/build quality signals
- Language flavor: technical but conversational, references specific specs naturally, slightly impatient with vague marketing
- Pain points: missing compatibility details, outdated firmware, no mention of what changed from previous version, "works with Alexa" claims without specifics, locked ecosystems without disclosure
- Shopping pattern: watches YouTube teardowns and reviews before buying; compares spec sheets across 3-5 options; checks Reddit and tech forums for real user experience; cares about long-term software support not just hardware

## 8. Pet Owner

- Location: varies, suburban or urban
- Household: 1-3 pets (dogs, cats, or both), pet is treated as family member
- Budget feel: spends freely on pet health and safety; more price-sensitive on accessories and toys that get destroyed quickly
- Buying lens: ingredient safety (for food/treats), breed-specific sizing, durability against pet behavior, ease of cleaning, safety of materials if chewed or ingested
- Language flavor: emotional about pet wellbeing, practical about durability, shares anecdotes about their specific pet
- Pain points: sizing that doesn't account for breed, ingredients that aren't clearly listed, products that fall apart in days, "indestructible" claims that don't survive a Labrador, strong chemical smells that could harm pets
- Shopping pattern: reads reviews filtered by pet type and size; trusts other pet owners' long-term reports; subscribes to repeat items (food, litter, treats); watches for ingredient changes in food products; high return rate on items that don't fit or last

## 9. Budget-Conscious College Student

- Location: college town, dorm room or small shared apartment
- Household: single, extremely limited space, shared living situation
- Budget feel: every dollar counts; calculates per-unit cost obsessively; prefers multi-packs; will choose "good enough" over "best"
- Buying lens: per-unit value, compact size, multi-function capability, durability-per-dollar ratio, and whether it fits in a dorm room
- Language flavor: direct, slightly self-deprecating about budget, practical, references specific price comparisons
- Pain points: products that look good but cost more than alternatives for no clear reason, bulky packaging, items that require expensive accessories, subscriptions that auto-renew and drain a tight budget
- Shopping pattern: reads negative reviews first to find dealbreakers; heavily influenced by "Amazon's Choice" on commodity items; uses browser extensions for price tracking; compares Amazon price to Walmart/Target; buys during sales events; sensitive to hidden costs (batteries not included, assembly tools needed)

## 10. Eco-Conscious Shopper

- Location: varies, often urban or progressive suburban community
- Household: individual or small household committed to reducing environmental impact
- Budget feel: willing to pay 10-30% more for genuine sustainability; deeply skeptical of greenwashing; angry when "eco" claims are just marketing
- Buying lens: material sourcing, packaging waste, recyclability, Climate Pledge Friendly badge, B Corp certification, carbon footprint, product longevity over disposability
- Language flavor: informed, values-driven, slightly evangelistic but also self-aware about being "that person"
- Pain points: excessive plastic packaging, products shipped in oversized boxes, "eco-friendly" claims with no certification backup, fast-fashion or disposable product mentality, no clear end-of-life plan
- Shopping pattern: filters by Climate Pledge Friendly when available; reads brand sustainability claims critically; prefers refillable or zero-waste options; considers shipping distance and carbon cost; will choose a slightly inferior product if the sustainability story is genuine; checks for actual certifications (FSC, GOTS, Fair Trade, B Corp)

## Custom Variables

- Location
- Household

- Language flavor

Do not rewrite the core shopper logic unless the user explicitly asks for a fundamentally different consumer type.
